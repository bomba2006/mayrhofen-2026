Mayrhofen · Austrian Alps

A hiking trip through the Zillertal Valley, Tyrol, Austria
August 31 – September 8, 2026 · 3 adults
✈️ Fly to Innsbruck (1hr) · Munich or Salzburg (2.5hrs) · 🚗 Rent a car · 🏠 Self-catering

We're spending nine days in Mayrhofen — a mountain town at 633 meters in the Austrian Alps. Each day we hike a different trail. By the end, we'll have walked through wildflower meadows, alongside thundering waterfalls, across a high suspension bridge over turquoise water, and onto a glacier at 3,250 meters with snow under our feet in summer. And we'll have coffee and strudel at a mountain hut every single day.

The Hikes

From easiest to most challenging — every hike includes a strudel stop at a mountain hut

Stilluptal Waterfall Valley

Gentle15km
A long, flat walk through a valley of waterfalls. Boardwalks wind past dozens of cascades. The easiest hike — perfect for a relaxing day. Falls are MORE dramatic when it rains.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel at the Stilluppe rest stop

Penken Panorama Trail

Gentle8.7km
Gondola to 1,800m, then a circular walk with 360° views of the Zillertal Alps. Almost flat. Perfect first-day hike to ease in.
☕🥮 Kaiserschmarrn (Austrian pancake dessert) at Grillhofalm

Filzenkogel

Gentle7km
Short summit hike from the Ahorn gondola. Quick climb to a beautiful viewpoint. Back in time for a relaxed afternoon.
☕🥮 Strudel at Ahornhütte

Ahorn High Walk

Moderate8km
Ahornbahn gondola to nearly 2,000m, then walk alpine terrain to Edelhütte at 2,238m. Strudel with a view of Ahornspitze peak — without climbing to the summit.
☕🥮 Strudel at Edelhütte — 2,238 meters, with summit views

Wolfsklamm Gorge

Moderate3km
350 wooden steps through a narrow gorge with waterfalls rushing beside you. Cross bridges and climb staircases bolted into rock walls. Short, spectacular — one of the Alps' most beautiful gorges.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel in the village of Stans

Berliner Hütte

Steady18.3km
Long day walking up a beautiful valley to a historic 1879 mountain hut. The path climbs gradually through wildflower meadows — never steep, just long. Coffee and cake earned by the climb.
☕🥮 Coffee & cake at Berliner Hütte — a historic alpine hut from 1879

Olpererhütte

Challenging13.8km
The most famous hike in the Zillertal. Drive to a turquoise reservoir, climb steadily to a hut at 2,389m. The highlight: a suspension bridge spanning a glacial stream with the turquoise lake below. The most photographed viewpoint in the valley. Our one big day.
☕🥮 Strudel at Olpererhütte — on the famous bridge

Krimml Waterfalls

Day Trip
Scenic drive over a mountain pass to Europe's highest waterfall — 385 meters in three tiers. Walk the boardwalk up alongside the falls.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel at Krimml café

Hintertux Glacier

Day Trip
Three gondolas to 3,250m — onto a glacier with snow year-round. Walk on ice, enjoy the views, strudel at the mountain restaurant. Works in ANY weather — our ultimate backup plan.
☕🥮 Strudel at Tuxer Fernerhaus — with snow outside in summer

Is It Safe? What If It Rains?

All hikes are on well-marked, maintained trails. Gondolas skip the steepest sections — we start high with less climbing. Hikes range from gentle valley walks to steady climbs. Only Olpererhütte is more challenging, but spread over 14km — a gradual climb, not a wall.

Every day has a rain backup. Hintertux Glacier works in any weather. Waterfall trails are better in rain. And we can swap days — if Thursday storms, do Thursday's hike Saturday.
