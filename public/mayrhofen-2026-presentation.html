Mayrhofen · Austrian Alps

A hiking trip through the Zillertal Valley

Each day we'll hike a different trail — through wildflower meadows, along high ridges, into waterfall gorges, and up to a glacier. We'll have coffee and strudel at a mountain hut every single day. The Hikes

From easiest to most challenging

Stilluptal Waterfall Valley

Gentle15km
A long, flat walk through a valley full of waterfalls. Wooden boardwalks wind past dozens of cascades. The easiest hike of the trip — perfect for a relaxing day. The falls are even more dramatic when it rains.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel at the Stilluppe mountain rest stop

Penken Panorama Trail

Gentle8.7km
We take the gondola up to 1,800 meters and walk a circular trail with 360° views of the entire Zillertal Alps. Short, scenic, and almost flat. The perfect first-day hike to ease into the mountains.
☕🥮 Kaiserschmarrn (Austrian pancake dessert) at Grillhofalm hut

Filzenkogel

Gentle7km
A short summit hike starting from the Ahorn gondola. Quick climb to a beautiful viewpoint over the Zillertal main ridge. Back in time for a relaxed afternoon.
☕🥮 Strudel at Ahornhütte

Ahorn High Walk

Moderate8km
Take the Ahornbahn gondola to nearly 2,000 meters, then walk through high alpine terrain to the Edelhütte at 2,238 meters. We'll have strudel with a view of the Ahornspitze peak — without having to climb all the way to the summit.
☕🥮 Strudel at Edelhütte — 2,238 meters up, with summit views

Wolfsklamm Gorge

Moderate3km
350 wooden steps climb through a narrow gorge with waterfalls rushing beside you. Cross wooden bridges and climb staircases bolted into the rock walls. Short but spectacular — one of the most beautiful gorges in the Alps.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel at a café in the village of Stans

Berliner Hütte

Steady18.3km
A long day walking up a beautiful valley to a historic mountain hut built in 1879. The path climbs gradually through wildflower meadows — never steep, just long. The reward is coffee and cake at the hut, earned by the climb.
☕🥮 Coffee & cake at the Berliner Hütte — a historic alpine hut from 1879

Olpererhütte

Challenging13.8km
The most famous hike in the Zillertal Valley. We drive to a turquoise mountain reservoir, then climb steadily to a hut perched at 2,389 meters. The highlight is a suspension bridge over a glacial stream with the turquoise lake far below. The view has been photographed thousands of times — and for good reason. This is our one big day.
☕🥮 Strudel at the Olpererhütte — on the famous suspension bridge

Krimml Waterfalls

Day Trip
A scenic drive over a mountain pass to see Europe's highest waterfall — 385 meters of thundering water in three tiers. We'll walk the boardwalk path up alongside the falls. The spray is said to be good for the lungs.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel at the Krimml café

Hintertux Glacier

Day Trip
Three gondola rides take us up to 3,250 meters — above the clouds, onto a glacier where there's snow year-round. We can walk on the glacier, see the views, and warm up with strudel at the mountain restaurant. Works in any weather — our backup plan if it rains.
☕🥮 Strudel at Tuxer Fernerhaus — with snow outside, even in summer

Zillertal High Road

Scenic Drive
A 50-kilometer drive along one of Austria's most beautiful alpine roads, climbing to 2,020 meters. We'll stop at viewpoints and mountain huts along the way. No hiking required — pure scenery.

We have backup plans for rainy days

September in the Alps is beautiful but changeable. Mornings are usually clear — we'll start our hikes early. Afternoon rain is possible, so we always pack a rain shell. The Hintertux Glacier is our weather-proof backup: it works in any conditions.
