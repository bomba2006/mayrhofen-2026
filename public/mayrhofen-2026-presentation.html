Olpererhütte

129|129| Challenging13.8km 130|130|
The most famous hike in the Zillertal Valley. We drive to a turquoise mountain reservoir, then climb steadily to a hut perched at 2,389 meters. The highlight is a suspension bridge over a glacial stream with the turquoise lake far below. The view has been photographed thousands of times — and for good reason. This is our one big day.

🌧️

Rain possible

We have backup plans for rainy days

September in the Alps is beautiful but changeable. Mornings are usually clear — we'll start our hikes early. Afternoon rain is possible, so we always pack a rain shell. The Hintertux Glacier is our weather-proof backup: it works in any conditions.

Is It Safe? What If It Rains?

All our hikes are on well-marked, maintained trails. We use gondolas to skip the steepest sections — so we start each hike already high up, with less climbing. The hikes range from gentle valley walks to steady climbs. Only one day is more challenging (Olpererhütte), and even that is spread over 14 kilometers — a gradual climb, not a steep wall.

We have a rain backup for every single day. The Hintertux Glacier works in any weather. Several waterfall trails are actually better in the rain. And we can swap days around — if Thursday is stormy, we do Thursday's hike on Saturday instead.
