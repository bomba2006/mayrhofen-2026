📍 Overview

Mayrhofen (6290), Zillertal Valley, Tyrol, Austria. Elevation ~630m. Surrounded by Zillertal Alps Nature Park with ~80 glaciers, 3 reservoirs. 532 km hiking trails, 1400+ km in Zillertal. 10 summer lifts. Hintertux Glacier year-round skiing to 3250m. Zillertalbahn Jenbach-Mayrhofen every 30 min. Innsbruck 1h 15m by train.

Group: 3 adults · Style: Basecamp model, self-catering apartment · Transport: Car for drives outside valley

Hiking

532 km trails in Mayrhofen, 1400+ km in Zillertal. Huts open June–September.

Must-do hikes:

  • Olpererhütte via Schlegeis Reservoir — Instagram-famous suspension bridge, turquoise water, 4.8★ AllTrails, moderate
  • Berliner Höhenweg — multi-day classic, challenging
  • Stilluptal Valley Walk — easy, waterfalls, reservoir
  • Penken Panorama Trails — gondola up, ridge walks, 360° views

🌤️ Weather & Packing

September Climate

Temperatures 4–15°C (40–58°F) in valley. Colder at altitude. Sub-zero at glacier. Moderately chilly, humid, possible rain. Transitional season.

Packing List

  • Layers: fleece, lightweight puffy, waterproof rain shell
  • Waterproof hiking boots (broken in)
  • Hiking trousers (not jeans)
  • Hat, gloves, sunglasses, sunscreen
  • Swimwear
  • Daypack with rain cover

🌱 Vegan Food Guide

Reality check: Traditional Austrian hut food is NOT vegan. Kaiserschmarrn = eggs+milk. Strudel = butter. Goulash = meat. ☕ Coffee IS vegan. Strategy: pack trail lunches, use huts for coffee + fries/rösti.

💬 Community Forum Intel

Key Consensus

  1. Olpererhütte: Universally loved. Parking nightmare if you're late. Go before 8am.
  2. Ahornspitze: Fantastic but don't do it in rain. Exposed. Never alone.
  3. Berliner Höhenweg: More technical than stats suggest. Not for casual hikers.
  4. Lifts: Everyone recommends using them. Valley-to-peak grind is not the Zillertal way.
  5. Activcard: Good value IF used most days. One-ride-per-day limit frustrates people.
  6. September: Shoulder season is great but not empty — crowds linger into mid-September.
