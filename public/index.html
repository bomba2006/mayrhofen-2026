Stilluptal Waterfall ValleyGentle15km
Long, flat walk through a valley of waterfalls. Boardwalks wind past dozens of cascades. The easiest hike — perfect for a relaxing day.
📏 15.3km📈 325m gain⚡ 21m/km (flattest)⏱ 5h⭐ 4.5★ (900+ reviews)
Waterfalls are more dramatic in rain — this is a rare hike that's better in bad weather. Drive past the Schlegeis reservoir turnoff into the Stilluptal valley. Trail follows the river past cascades of all sizes. Wooden boardwalks keep feet dry. Return along the same path or loop via the forest road.
Alternative rejected: Zemmbach valley walk — also flat but shorter and with fewer waterfalls.
☕🥮 Strudel at the Stilluppe rest stop, mid-valley · €8-12 for coffee + cake
Sources: AllTrails (4.5★), Komoot, Google Maps
Penken Panorama TrailGentle8.7km
Gondola to 1,800m, then a circular walk with 360° Zillertal Alps views. Almost flat. Perfect first-day hike to ease into the mountains.
📏 8.7km📈 300m gain⚡ 35m/km (gentle)⏱ 2.5-3h⭐ 4.6★ (1,300+ reviews)
Take Penkenbahn gondola from Mayrhofen to 1,800m. Circular trail loops around the Penken plateau — views of the Zillertal main ridge, Hintertux Glacier in the distance, and the valley below. Multiple huts along the route. Descend back via gondola or walk down.
Alternative rejected: Penken Full Loop — longer route with same views, not worth the extra distance at this gentle gradient.
☕🥮 Kaiserschmarrn at Grillhofalm · 1,800m · the iconic Austrian pancake dessert
Sources: mayrhofen.at, AllTrails (4.6★), Google Maps
FilzenkogelGentle7km
Short summit hike from the Ahorn gondola. Quick climb to a beautiful viewpoint over the Zillertal main ridge. Back by early afternoon.
📏 7km📈 350m gain⚡ 50m/km⏱ 2-3h⭐ 4.4★ (320+ reviews)
Take Ahornbahn to 1,978m. A steady climb up Filzenkogel — the smallest of the Zillertal peaks but with spectacular views. Good spacer day between longer hikes. Return to the Ahorn top station for strudel.
☕🥮 Strudel at Ahornhütte · 2,000m · views of the Ahornspitze massif
Sources: Komoot, Outdooractive, Google Maps
Wolfsklamm GorgeModerate3km
350 wooden steps through a narrow gorge with waterfalls. Cross bridges and climb staircases bolted into rock. One of the Alps' most beautiful gorges.
📏 ~3km📈 ~200m⚡ stairs⏱ 1.5-2h⭐ 4.6★ (450+ reviews)
Located in Stans, 15-min drive from Mayrhofen. The gorge walk climbs 350 wooden steps through narrow rock walls with the Stanserbach waterfall rushing beside you. €6 entry. Combine with Zillertal High Road for a full day — gorge in the morning, scenic drive in the afternoon.
Alternatives rejected: Tiefenbachklamm and Kaiserklamm — Wolfsklamm rated highest and closest to Mayrhofen.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel in the village of Stans · several cafés at the gorge exit
Sources: TripAdvisor (4.6★), Google Maps, wanderkompass.de
Ahorn High WalkModerate8km
Ahornbahn gondola to nearly 2,000m, then walk high alpine terrain to Edelhütte at 2,238m. Strudel with Ahornspitze views — without the summit climb.
📏 8km📈 400m gain⚡ 50m/km⏱ 3-4h⭐ 4.5★ (700+ reviews)
Start at Ahornbahn top (1,978m). Walk through alpine meadows to Edelhütte (2,238m), a mountain hut with a terrace facing Ahornspitze peak. You get the same view as summit climbers without the exposed ridge and 81m/km steepness. Loop back to the gondola. One of the highest strudel stops on the trip.
Alternative rejected: Ahornspitze summit (81m/km, exposed ridge with via ferrata sections). Same mountain, same hut, none of the risk.
☕🥮 Strudel at Edelhütte · 2,238m · the highest strudel on the trip · views of Ahornspitze
Sources: AllTrails (4.5★), Komoot, bergbahn-mayrhofen.at
Berliner HütteSteady18.3km
Long day walking up a valley to a historic 1879 hut. Gradual climb through wildflower meadows — never steep, just long. Coffee and cake earned.
📏 18.3km📈 854m gain⚡ 47m/km (gradual)⏱ 6.5-7h⭐ 4.7★ (850+ reviews)
The longest hike. Start at Breitlahner (1,200m) in the Zemmgrund valley. The path follows the Zemmbach river upward through wildflower meadows and past alpine pastures. The hut at 2,042m is one of the oldest DAV huts in Austria, built 1879. At 47m/km, it's less steep than our Salzburg Day 4 (50m/km) — the difficulty is the distance, not the gradient. Return same route.
Alternative rejected: Friesenberghaus — steeper approach, fewer reviews, less historic hut.
☕🥮 Coffee & cake at Berliner Hütte · 2,042m · historic 1879 DAV hut
Sources: AllTrails (4.7★), DAV Berliner Hütte website, Google Maps
OlpererhütteChallenging13.8km
The #1 hike in Zillertal (4.8★, 1,674+ reviews). Turquoise reservoir, suspension bridge, hut at 2,389m. Our one big day — and the reason we chose Mayrhofen.
📏 13.8km📈 876m gain⚡ 63m/km (steady)⏱ 5.5h⭐ 4.8★ (1,674+ reviews)
Drive the Schlegeis toll road (€18, 8km of switchbacks) to the reservoir at 1,800m. The trail climbs steadily along the lake, then zigzags up to the hut at 2,389m. The highlight: a suspension bridge spanning a glacial stream with turquoise Schlegeis reservoir far below. The most photographed viewpoint in the Zillertal Valley. Loop back via the other side of the reservoir.
Alternative rejected: Olpererhütte short route (94m/km — the steepest option in our entire inventory). The full loop is both gentler AND more scenic — you miss the bridge on the short route.
Weather: needs clear or partly cloudy. If it storms, swap with Saturday's glacier day. Arrive before 8am — the Schlegeis parking fills fast.
☕🥮 Strudel at Olpererhütte · 2,389m · on the famous suspension bridge terrace
Sources: AllTrails (4.8★), Google Maps, Komoot, TripAdvisor forum threads
Krimml WaterfallsDay Trip
Europe's highest waterfall — 385m in three tiers. Scenic drive over a mountain pass, then walk the boardwalk up alongside the falls.
📏 4km walk📈 ~350m⏱ 2h⭐ 4.7★ (12,000+ reviews)
1-1.5 hour drive from Mayrhofen via Gerlos Pass (€12 toll). The falls drop 385m in three tiers — the highest in Europe. Well-maintained boardwalk climbs alongside the cascades. The spray is legendary ("good for the lungs"). Multiple viewing platforms at each tier. Falls are most powerful after rain — another hike that works in bad weather.
Alternative rejected: Stuibenfall (closer, near Ötz — smaller, fewer reviews). Krimml is the world-class option.
☕🥮 Coffee & strudel at Krimml café at the base · entry €9
Sources: Google Maps (4.7★, 12K+), krimmler-wasserfaelle.at
Hintertux GlacierDay Trip
Three gondolas to 3,250m — year-round snow. Walk on ice, enjoy the panorama, strudel at the top. Works in ANY weather. Our backup plan.
📏 3 gondolas up📈 3,250m summit⏱ half-day⭐ 4.6★ (4,500+ reviews)
25-min drive to Hintertux base. Three cable cars take you from 1,500m to 3,250m — the only year-round accessible glacier in Austria. At the top: panoramic terrace over the Alps, snow even in September, and the Tuxer Fernerhaus restaurant. The gondolas are enclosed — it works in ANY weather. This is our universal rain backup. Can be done in a half-day.
Alternative rejected: Kitzsteinhorn glacier near Kaprun — further drive, smaller accessible area.
☕🥮 Strudel at Tuxer Fernerhaus · 3,250m · snow outside in summer
Sources: Google Maps (4.6★), hintertuxergletscher.at

Evidence — Why These Choices?

Why Mayrhofen Over Other Alpine Towns?

🚡

Two summer gondolas

Penkenbahn + Ahornbahn. Most towns have one — double the high-access without climbing.

💧

Three gorges nearby

Wolfsklamm, Stilluptal, Krimml all ≤30min. Zell am See has only one gorge.

⭐

#1 rated Zillertal hike

Olpererhütte (4.8★, 1,674 reviews) — only accessible from this valley.

🛒

Sunday supermarket

MPREIS open 7:30-19:00 Sundays — Zell am See and Schladming close Sundays.

Sources: TripAdvisor forums (2016-2026), mayrhofen.at, zillertal.at, Google Maps hours verified Jun 2026
